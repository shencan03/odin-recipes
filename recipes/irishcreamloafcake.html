Irish Cream Loaf Cake

Description

This Irish cream loaf cake is studded with chocolate chips and topped with an Irish cream frosting. The loaf comes out moist but crumbly and almost feels like a cross between a cake and a soft cookie.

Ingredients

Cake

  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 1/2 cup butter, melted, plus more for greasing the pan
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup Irish cream liqueur (such as Baileys®)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup semi sweet chocolate chips

Frosting

  • 3 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tablespoon Irish cream liqueur (such as Baileys®)
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1 tablespoons milk, or more to taste

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Lightly grease and line an 8x4-inch loaf pan with parchment paper.
  2. Whisk together sugar and melted butter in a large bowl until smooth. Beat in eggs until fully combined. Stir in Irish cream liqueur and vanilla.
  3. Fold in flour, baking powder, and salt just until combined; do not overmix. Stir in chocolate chips. Spread batter evenly into the prepared loaf pan.
  4. Bake until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean, 40 to 45 minutes.
  5. Cool loaf in the pan for 15 minutes, then remove from the pan and cool completely on a wire rack.
  6. Prepare the frosting: Combine cream cheese, powdered sugar, Irish cream liqueur, and salt in a bowl. Beat with an electric mixer on low speed until smooth, adding milk as needed to make a spreading consistency. Spread frosting over the cooled loaf. Slice and serve.
